# EuroLeague Basketball Teams - Technology Products Mapping

*Research compiled: January 2026*  
*Scope: Officially confirmed technology products only*

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive document maps the officially confirmed technology products used by EuroLeague basketball teams across all categories including wearables, video analysis, recovery tools, data analytics platforms, and facility technology. Information is organized by team and focuses on team-wide usage, with athlete-specific tools included where confirmed.

**Key Findings:**
- 14 out of 18 EuroLeague teams have at least one publicly confirmed technology product
- KINEXON leads with 4 team partnerships, followed by multiple specialized providers
- Performance tracking and wearables are the most common category (9 teams)
- EuroLeague has approved in-game use of wearables for real-time data collection
- Industry trends show increasing adoption of AI-powered video analysis and load management systems

## Overview

EuroLeague basketball teams primarily use wearable sensors, tracking systems, and analytics software for performance monitoring, with adoption varying by club. Specific tech products are documented for a subset of teams, focusing on tools for coaches (analytics/tactics), athletes (wearables/load tracking), executives (management platforms), physios, and strength & conditioning staff (recovery/injury prevention).

**Common Technology Categories:**
1. **Performance Tracking & Wearables** - GPS, IMU sensors, load monitoring
2. **Video Analysis** - AI-powered game and training footage analysis
3. **Data Analytics Platforms** - Integrated performance and fan data systems
4. **Recovery & Medical** - Injury prevention, rehabilitation equipment
5. **Facility Technology** - Smart courts, interactive surfaces
6. **Fan Engagement** - Digital authentication, mobile apps

### Current Adoption Patterns

1. **Wearable Technology Dominance**: Most technologically advanced teams (9 out of 14 confirmed) utilize wearable sensors for daily training load management.

2. **KINEXON Market Leadership**: KINEXON has emerged as the leading provider with 4 confirmed partnerships, offering comprehensive solutions from tracking to AI video analysis.

3. **Video Analysis Growth**: 6 teams have adopted AI-powered video analysis tools, representing a shift toward automated coaching assistance.

4. **Integrated Platforms**: Teams increasingly seek platforms that combine multiple data sources (wearables, video, medical records) into unified systems.

5. **Real-Time Data**: Shift from post-session analysis to real-time monitoring during training and games, enabled by EuroLeague's approval of in-game wearables.

## Appendix: Technology Definitions

### Performance Tracking Technologies

**GPS (Global Positioning System):**
- Satellite-based tracking for outdoor positioning
- Measures: Speed, distance, acceleration, deceleration
- Typical accuracy: 5-10 Hz sampling rate

**IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit):**
- Accelerometer and gyroscope-based tracking
- Works indoors and outdoors
- Measures: Acceleration, rotation, changes of direction, jumps
- Typical accuracy: 100+ Hz sampling rate

**LPS (Local Positioning System):**
- Fixed sensors create indoor positioning network
- Real-time 3D tracking
- Measures: X, Y, Z coordinates, speed, distance between players
- Typical accuracy: Sub-second latency, centimeter precision

**Optical Tracking:**
- Camera-based player and ball tracking
- Computer vision algorithms
- Measures: Player positions, ball trajectory, spatial relationships
- Used for: Broadcast enhancement, tactical analysis

### Video Analysis Categories

**AI-Powered Analysis:**
- Automatic detection of events (shots, passes, defensive actions)
- Computer vision for player and ball recognition
- Automated tagging and highlight creation

**Manual Tagging Systems:**
- Coach-driven video markup
- Custom event definition
- Integration with performance data

**Automatic Camera Systems:**
- Robotic or fixed panoramic cameras
- Cloud upload and storage
- Mobile access for coaches

### Load Management Metrics

**External Load:**
- Physical demands measured by GPS/IMU
- Metrics: Distance, speed, acceleration, decelerations, jumps
- Objective measure of what athlete does

**Internal Load:**
- Physiological response to training
- Metrics: Heart rate, heart rate variability, RPE
- Subjective/objective measure of how athlete responds

**TRIMP (Training Impulse):**
- Integration of heart rate and duration
- Quantifies cardiovascular stress
- Used for training dose monitoring

**Recovery Metrics:**
- Sleep quality and duration
- Resting heart rate variability
- Muscle soreness and fatigue indicators
- Readiness scores
