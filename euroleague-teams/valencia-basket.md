**Category:** Strength & Conditioning / Biomechanics  
**Vendor:** KangaTech (Australia-based sports technology company)  
**Product:** KT360 isokinetic training rig

**Confirmation Source:**  
- euroleague-tech-products.md database entry
- Announced: July 2025
- **Milestone**: Valencia Basket identified as "First European basketball team to adopt this system"

**Technology Details:**  
- **Hardware**: 7-load-cell 360° rotational device measuring players' maximal push/pull force
- **Force Measurement**: Up to 200 kg capacity across multiple directional axes
- **Assessment Capabilities**: Maximal strength profiling, bilateral asymmetry detection, joint-specific force production, rotational power output
- **Training Modes**: Isokinetic resistance (constant speed variable resistance), eccentric overload protocols, unilateral strength testing
- **Data Output**: Force curves, power metrics, strength imbalance reports, longitudinal tracking

**Valencia Basket Use Cases:**
1. **Strength Assessment**: Baseline testing of all players (pre-season, mid-season checkpoints) to establish individual strength profiles
2. **Injury Rehabilitation**: Post-injury strength recovery tracking (e.g., ACL return-to-play requires 90%+ bilateral symmetry)
3. **Strength Prescriptions**: S&C staff use KT360 data to prescribe individualized strength programs (target weak muscle groups, address asymmetries)
4. **Injury Risk Management**: Identify players with strength deficits or asymmetries that correlate with injury risk (e.g., quadriceps imbalance → knee injury risk)
5. **Performance Benchmarking**: Track strength progression over season, compare players at similar positions

**Strategic Rationale:**  
- **European Pioneer Status**: Being "First European basketball team" creates competitive advantage (1-2 year head start on strength data insights vs. competitors)
- **Injury History Context**: Valencia Basket may have experienced injury issues prompting investment in strength assessment technology
- **Mid-Tier Budget Optimization**: KT360 focuses investment on strength/conditioning vs. more expensive LPS or automated video systems
- **Performance Staff Expertise**: Hiring strength coach with isokinetic training background likely preceded KT360 adoption
- **EuroCup to Euroleague Transition**: 2023 EuroCup championship earned Euroleague spot, KT360 investment signals commitment to compete at higher level

**Estimated Investment:**  
- **Hardware Purchase**: €40-80K one-time (KT360 rig + installation)
- **Annual Support**: €5-15K/year (software updates, calibration, maintenance)
- **Total Year 1**: €45-95K
- **Ongoing**: €5-15K/year recurring

**Technology Leadership:**  
- Valencia Basket **first mover** in European basketball for isokinetic strength assessment technology
- KT360 more common in Australian sports (rugby, AFL), NBA (limited adoption ~5-10 teams), European basketball largely unexplored
- Positions Valencia as strength/conditioning innovator vs. traditional wearables/video focus
