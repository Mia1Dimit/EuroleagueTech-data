## Lymphatic Drainage Therapy

### Mechanism

**Lymphatic System**:
- Network of vessels/nodes that transport lymph (fluid with immune cells, waste products)
- Muscles/breathing drive lymph flow (no pump like heart for blood)
- **Stagnant lymph** → swelling (edema), slower recovery, waste accumulation

**Lymphatic Drainage Goal**:
- **Stimulate lymph flow** → reduce swelling, remove metabolic waste, enhance tissue oxygenation
- **Methods**: Manual massage (MLD) or mechanical compression (pressotherapy)

**Sports Applications**:
- **Post-exercise recovery**: Remove lactate, inflammatory mediators
- **Injury management**: Reduce swelling from sprains, contusions, surgery
- **Travel**: Reduce leg swelling from long flights (basketball teams travel extensively)

### Evidence

**Research**:
- **Pressotherapy**: Some studies show reduced muscle soreness, swelling (benefits moderate)
- **Manual lymphatic drainage**: Evidence mixed (benefits for post-surgery, chronic lymphedema)
- **vs. Compression garments**: Active compression (pressotherapy) more effective than passive (sleeves)

**Clinical Use**:
- Widely used in European sports medicine (Italy, Spain, France)
- Common in soccer, cycling, basketball (recovery modality)
