## Company Overview

**ASB Systembau** is a German sports flooring manufacturer specializing in high-performance playing surfaces for basketball, volleyball, handball, and multi-purpose arenas. The company's flagship innovation, **ASB GlassFloor**, transforms the entire basketball court into a **massive LED display** capable of projecting team logos, animations, sponsor content, and interactive games—while maintaining FIBA-approved playing characteristics (traction, shock absorption, ball bounce).

### Market Position

**Global Installations**: 50+ worldwide (primarily multi-purpose arenas, corporate venues, flagship stores)
- **Sports**: Basketball, volleyball, handball, futsal, concerts, trade shows

## Technology Specifications

### Hardware: LED-Embedded Playing Surface

**Construction**:
- **Base layer**: Traditional wood sports floor (beech or maple)
- **LED modules**: 450,000 individual LEDs embedded in modular panels (60cm × 60cm tiles)
- **Surface layer**: 12mm tempered glass with anti-slip coating
- **Total thickness**: 30mm (vs. 22mm traditional wood floor)

**LED Specifications**:
- **Resolution**: 60 pixels per square meter (high-definition for large-scale display)
- **Color depth**: 16.7 million colors (full RGB spectrum)
- **Brightness**: 1,000-2,500 nits (adjustable; dimmed during games to avoid player distraction)
- **Refresh rate**: 60Hz (smooth animations, no flickering)
- **Lifespan**: 50,000-100,000 hours (10-20 years with moderate use)

**Playing Characteristics** (FIBA Approved):
- **Traction**: Anti-slip coating (same grip as wood floor)
- **Shock absorption**: 45-55% force reduction (FIBA standard: >35%)
- **Ball bounce**: 90-96% return (FIBA standard: 90-110%)
- **Area deflection**: 2.3-3.5mm (FIBA standard: <5mm)

**Safety Certifications**:
- **FIBA Level 1**: Approved for official competitions (Olympics, FIBA World Cup, Euroleague)
- **EN 14904**: European sports floor standard
- **DIN 18032-2**: German sports facility standard

### Software: Content Management System (CMS)

**ASB Cloud Platform**:
- **Interface**: Web-based dashboard (accessible from laptop, tablet, smartphone)
- **Functions**:
  - **Pre-game shows**: Upload animations (team logos, player intros, light shows)
  - **Sponsor content**: Display sponsor ads, branded animations (halftime, timeouts)
  - **Live control**: Trigger content during game (intro music → logo animation, timeout → sponsor ad)
  - **Scheduling**: Pre-program content calendar (automate recurring content)
  - **Multi-language**: Support for multiple languages (important for international events)

**Content Types**:
- **Static images**: Team logos, sponsor logos, court markings
- **Animations**: Video playback (pre-recorded or live), 3D effects, particle systems
- **Interactive**: Touchscreen games (fan activations during halftime—requires additional sensors)
- **Tactical overlays**: Coaching diagrams, spacing guidelines (training mode only, not during official games per FIBA rules)

**Third-Party Integrations**:
- **Scoreboard sync**: Display score, time, stats on court (coordinate with center-hung videoboard)
- **Broadcast sync**: Trigger court animations based on TV broadcast cues (intro music, commercial breaks)
- **Mobile app integration**: Fans vote on halftime content via team app → court displays winning selection

## Competitive Advantages (Why ASB GlassFloor vs. Alternatives)

### vs. Traditional Wood Floor

**Traditional Floor**:
- **Cost**: €50-150k (basketball court installation)
- **Capabilities**: Static markings (painted lines, logos)
- **Lifespan**: 15-25 years (refinishing every 5-10 years)

**ASB GlassFloor Advantages**:
- **Dynamic content**: Change court appearance instantly (no repainting required)
- **Sponsor value**: Rotating sponsor content (sell multiple sponsors vs. single painted logo)
- **Event versatility**: Transform court for concerts, trade shows, corporate events (higher venue utilization)

**ASB GlassFloor Disadvantages**:
- **Cost**: 10-20x more expensive (€1-2M vs. €50-150k)
- **Maintenance**: Higher (€50-150k/year vs. €5-15k/year refinishing)
- **Complexity**: Requires technical staff (content creation, troubleshooting)

### vs. Projection Mapping (Floor Projectors)

**Projection Mapping**:
- **Type**: Overhead projectors beam images onto traditional floor
- **Cost**: €100-500k (projector array + installation)
- **Examples**: NBA All-Star introductions, opening ceremonies

**ASB GlassFloor Advantages**:
- **Brightness**: LEDs visible in full arena lighting (projectors require dimmed lights)
- **Durability**: LEDs embedded (protected by glass) vs. projectors (delicate, hanging from ceiling)
- **Portability**: Court is permanent (no setup/teardown) vs. projector array (complex rigging)

**Projection Mapping Advantages**:
- **Cost**: 2-4x cheaper than ASB GlassFloor
- **Flexibility**: Easier to update/replace projectors than rebuild LED court
- **Retrofit-friendly**: Works with existing floor (no demolition required)

**Market split**:
- **New construction / major renovation**: ASB GlassFloor (permanent solution)
- **Existing arenas, temporary events**: Projection mapping (retrofit without replacing floor)

### vs. Courtside LED Ribbons (Perimeter Displays)

**Perimeter LED**:
- **Type**: LED screens around court edge (baseline, sidelines)
- **Cost**: €100-400k
- **Capabilities**: Display ads, stats, sponsor content (but not on playing surface)

**ASB GlassFloor Unique Value**:
- **Playing surface as canvas**: Entire court (28m × 15m = 420 sq meters) vs. perimeter ribbons (100-150 sq meters)
- **Immersive**: Floor + walls + ceiling (complete visual environment)
- **Fan POV**: Overhead shots (TV broadcasts, social media) showcase floor graphics (perimeter ribbons less visible from above)

**Complementary, not competitive**: Most ASB GlassFloor installations ALSO have perimeter LED ribbons (Panathinaikos OAKA likely has both)

## Weaknesses

1. **High cost**: €1-2M initial (prohibitive for most Euroleague teams)
2. **Maintenance**: €50-150k/year (LED module failures, software support)
3. **Complexity**: Requires dedicated staff (content creation, technical operation)
4. **LED lifespan**: 10-20 years → €1M+ replacement cost eventually (vs. traditional floor 15-25 years with €5-15k refinishing)
5. **Distraction risk**: If LEDs too bright during play → player complaints (FIBA requires dimming, but still concern)
6. **Limited basketball ROI**: Performance impact = zero (purely entertainment/sponsorship value)
7. **Retrofit challenges**: Existing arenas require floor demolition, structural upgrades (electrical, cooling) → total project €1.5-3M

## Pricing & ROI

### Cost Breakdown

**Initial Investment**:
- **ASB GlassFloor hardware**: €800k-1.5M (450,000 LEDs, glass, installation)
- **Infrastructure upgrades**: €200-500k (electrical capacity, cooling systems, structural reinforcement)
- **Content creation**: €100-300k (first-year animations, branding packages, sponsor templates)
- **Training & support**: €20-50k (staff training, on-site technical assistance)
- **Total Year 1**: €1.1-2.3M

**Annual Operating Costs**:
- **Maintenance**: €50-150k (LED module replacements ~2-5% per year, software updates, technical support)
- **Content updates**: €20-80k (new animations, sponsor content, seasonal campaigns)
- **Electricity**: €10-30k (LED power consumption, cooling)
- **Total Annual**: €80-260k

### Return on Investment

**Sponsorship Revenue**:
- **Exclusive floor branding**: €300-500k/year (vs. €50-150k traditional courtside) → €150-350k incremental
- **Halftime sponsor activations**: €50-150k/year (interactive games, product demos)
- **Total**: €200-500k/year

**Event Rentals** (Non-Basketball):
- **Corporate events**: 5-10 per year × €50-150k = €250k-1.5M/year
- **Concerts**: 2-5 per year × €100-300k = €200k-1.5M/year (venue share of ticket sales)
- **Total**: €450k-3M/year (highly variable)

**Payback Period**:
- **Best case**: 1-2 years (high event utilization: €2-3M incremental revenue/year - €80-260k costs = €1.9-2.9M net → pays off €1.1-2.3M investment)
- **Moderate case**: 3-5 years (€500k-1M incremental revenue/year)
- **Worst case**: 10-20 years (basketball-only, minimal event rentals: €200-500k/year)

**Panathinaikos likely scenario**: Moderate to best case (OAKA hosts non-basketball events, sponsor value high in Greek market)

## Conclusion

ASB GlassFloor is a **premium interactive LED playing surface** that transforms basketball courts into dynamic canvases for entertainment, sponsorships, and events. With **1 confirmed Euroleague installation (Panathinaikos OAKA, 2024)** and a **€1-2M price tag**, ASB GlassFloor represents the **ultimate facilities technology investment**—delivering brand differentiation and sponsor value but requiring significant capital and high event utilization to justify ROI.

**Value Proposition**:
- **Unique differentiation**: Only 1/18 Euroleague teams has it (exclusive brand status)
- **Sponsor revenue**: €200-500k/year incremental (premium floor branding, halftime activations)
- **Event versatility**: Multi-purpose venue (basketball, concerts, corporate events)

**Limitations**:
- **High cost**: €1-2M initial + €80-260k/year operating (prohibitive for most teams)
- **ROI uncertainty**: Requires high event utilization (50+ non-basketball events/year) to justify
- **Zero performance impact**: Purely entertainment value (vs. wearables, video, analytics with on-court ROI)

**2028 Outlook**:
- **Euroleague adoption**: 2-4 teams (up from 1) as new arenas built and costs decrease
- **Cost reduction**: €500k-1M (economies of scale as global installations reach 200+)
- **Interactive features**: Touchscreen capabilities (halftime games, training drills)
- **Energy efficiency**: 50% electricity reduction (next-gen LEDs)

**Strategic Position**: **Luxury technology for elite teams** building new arenas or undergoing major renovations. Not essential (traditional floor sufficient for 95% teams), but powerful brand differentiator for teams competing for global fan attention and premium sponsorships.
