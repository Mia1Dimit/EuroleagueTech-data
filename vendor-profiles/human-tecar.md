## How TECAR Therapy Works

### Mechanism

**Energy Delivery**:
- Device generates **radiofrequency electrical current**
- Energy transferred into tissues via applicators
- Tissues resist the current → **endogenous heat production** (heat from inside tissues, not external)
- Heat increases cellular metabolism, blood flow, tissue oxygenation

**Therapeutic Effects Claimed**:
- **Pain relief**: Reduced pain perception (gate control theory, endorphin release)
- **Inflammation reduction**: Improved circulation removes inflammatory mediators
- **Tissue repair**: Increased cellular metabolism → faster healing
- **Muscle relaxation**: Heat relaxes tight/spasmed muscles

### Treatment Protocol

**Typical Session**:
- Duration: 15-30 minutes per area
- Frequency: 2-3 sessions/week for acute injuries, daily for chronic conditions
- Physiotherapist moves applicator over injured area
- Patient feels warmth/tingling (comfortable, not painful)

**Indications**:
- Muscle strains, contusions
- Tendinitis (Achilles, patellar, rotator cuff)
- Ligament sprains (ankle, knee)
- Back pain, joint pain
- Post-surgery rehabilitation

## Technology Differentiation

### TECAR vs. Ultrasound Therapy

**TECAR**:
- Radiofrequency energy (deeper penetration claimed)
- Endogenous heat (heat from within tissues)
- Larger treatment area per session
- More expensive equipment

**Ultrasound**:
- Sound waves (1-3 MHz)
- Variable depth (frequency-dependent)
- Smaller treatment area (ultrasound head ~5cm diameter)
- Lower cost, more widely available

**Both**: Used for similar conditions (tendinitis, muscle strains, pain relief)

### TECAR vs. Electrical Stimulation (TENS, EMS)

**TECAR**:
- Heat-based therapy (thermal effects)
- Tissue repair focus

**TENS/EMS** (like Compex):
- Muscle/nerve stimulation (electrical pulses)
- Pain relief (TENS) or muscle activation/recovery (EMS)

**Different mechanisms**, but both used in sports medicine
