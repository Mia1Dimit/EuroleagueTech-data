## Company Overview

**SDI Sport Floor** (SDi Sportfloor) is a Spanish sports flooring specialist based in the Basque Country, providing **premium hardwood basketball courts**, multi-sport surfaces, and portable court systems for professional clubs, national federations, and municipal sports facilities across Spain and Europe.

## Technology & Product Portfolio

### 1. Premium Hardwood Basketball Courts (Primary Product)

**Materials**:
- **Maple Hardwood**: North American hard maple (industry standard for professional basketball - NBA, Euroleague, FIBA)
- **Beech Hardwood**: European beech (alternative to maple, common in European sports facilities)
- **Parquet Construction**: Interlocking wood panels (60cm × 60cm typical) with tongue-and-groove joints

**Specifications** (FIBA-Approved):
- **Surface Finish**: Polyurethane coating (glossy or matte, depending on client preference)
- **Ball Bounce**: 90-96% return (FIBA standard: 90-110%)
- **Shock Absorption**: 45-55% force reduction (FIBA standard: >35% - protects player joints from impact)
- **Traction**: Anti-slip surface treatment (coefficient of friction optimized for basketball shoes)
- **Flatness**: <3mm deviation over 3-meter span (ensures consistent ball bounce, no uneven spots)

**Construction Layers** (Typical System):
1. **Subfloor**: Concrete or wood base (existing arena foundation)
2. **Vapor Barrier**: Moisture protection (prevents wood warping from humidity)
3. **Sleeper System**: Wood joists or rubber pads (creates air gap, enhances shock absorption)
4. **Plywood Underlayment**: Structural layer (distributes load, prevents panel sagging)
5. **Hardwood Surface**: Maple/beech parquet panels (playing surface layer)
6. **Finish Coating**: Polyurethane (2-4 coats, protects wood, provides grip)

**Installation Process**:
- **Timeline**: 5-10 days (depending on court size, complexity)
  - **Example**: Ampuero Municipal Sports Center (Cantabria) - 6 days for full court replacement (SDI case study)
- **Steps**: Subfloor preparation → sleeper installation → underlayment → hardwood panels → sanding → finish coats → line painting (3-point arc, free throw, center court)
- **Quality Control**: Flatness measurement, ball bounce testing, FIBA compliance verification

### 2. Multi-Sport Surfaces

**PVC Sports Flooring** (Secondary Product Line):
- **Material**: Vinyl/PVC synthetic surface (vs. hardwood)
- **Use Cases**: Multi-use gyms (basketball + volleyball + handball + indoor soccer), municipal sports centers (heavy traffic, lower maintenance)
- **Advantages**: Lower cost (€30-50K vs. €80-120K hardwood), faster installation (3-5 days), easier cleaning
- **Disadvantages**: Less premium feel, lower ball bounce consistency vs. hardwood
- **Client Example**: Ampuero Municipal Sports Center (Cantabria) - PVC court installed in 6 days

**Multi-Sport Line Configurations**:
- **Basketball FIBA Lines**: 3-point arc 6.75m, free throw 4.60m, restricted area 1.25m radius
- **Badminton Lines**: 13.4m × 6.1m court (Spanish Badminton Federation official provider)
- **Volleyball Lines**: 18m × 9m court (overlaid on basketball court for multi-use facilities)
- **Custom Branding**: Team logos at center court, sponsor logos (Euroleague teams), municipal crests (public facilities)

### 3. Portable Court Systems

**Event Courts** (Championship Deployments):
- **Use Case**: Euroleague Final Four, Copa ACB, Supercopa ACB (temporary courts installed over arena floors for major events)
- **Design**: Modular hardwood panels (60cm × 60cm tiles) with quick-connect joints
- **Installation**: 4-8 hours (vs. 5-10 days permanent court)
- **Transport**: Truck delivery, forklift assembly
- **Storage**: Warehouse storage between events (reusable for 10-20+ events)

**Advantages of Portable Courts**:
- **Arena Flexibility**: Install basketball court over ice rink (hockey arenas), concert stage, exhibition floor
- **Event Quality**: Same FIBA-approved hardwood as permanent courts (professional playing surface for championships)
- **Cost Efficiency**: Single portable court serves multiple events (Final Four 2024, 2025, 2026... vs. building permanent court at each venue)

**SDI Portable Court Experience**:
- **Euroleague Final Four**: Court provider for Euroleague championship (4-team tournament, 3 games over 3 days)
- **Copa ACB / Supercopa ACB**: Spanish Liga ACB championship events (portable courts installed in host city arenas)

**2. SDI vs. ASB GlassFloor (LED Court Technology)**

**ASB GlassFloor**:
- **Technology**: LED-embedded interactive court (450,000 LEDs beneath tempered glass)
- **Cost**: €1-2M (10-20x more expensive than SDI hardwood)
- **Euroleague Adoption**: 2 teams (Panathinaikos, Bayern Munich)

**SDI Advantages**:
- **Affordability**: €80-120K vs. €1-2M ASB (93% cheaper)

- **Lower Maintenance**: Hardwood refinishing €5-10K/year vs. ASB LED module replacements €50-150K/year
- **Market Reality**: 95%+ of professional basketball uses hardwood (ASB = niche luxury for mega-budgets)

**ASB Advantages**:
- **Fan Experience**: Pre-game LED shows, sponsor animations, interactive content (SDI = static court)
- **Differentiation**: Only 2 Euroleague teams have LED courts (exclusive technology)

**Conclusion**: SDI vs. ASB serve **different markets** - SDI = standard professional basketball (€20-50M budget clubs), ASB = luxury innovation (€50M+ mega-budgets + arena ownership).

**3. SDI vs. Synthetic Alternatives** (PVC, Rubber Sports Floors)

**Synthetic Floors**:
- **Cost**: €30-50K (cheaper than SDI hardwood €80-120K)
- **Use Cases**: Municipal gyms, schools, recreational facilities

**SDI Hardwood Advantages**:
- **Professional Standard**: FIBA requires hardwood for official competitions (synthetic NOT allowed for Euroleague, Liga ACB, international games)
- **Ball Bounce Consistency**: Hardwood 90-96% return (synthetic 80-90%, less predictable)
- **Player Preference**: Professional players train/play on hardwood (synthetic = practice facilities only, not game courts)

**Synthetic Advantages**:
- **Lower Cost**: €30-50K vs. €80-120K hardwood (40-60% cheaper)
- **Multi-Use Durability**: Synthetic withstands heavy traffic (concerts, events, non-basketball use) better than hardwood

**Conclusion**: Professional basketball clubs (Valencia Basket, Euroleague) MUST use hardwood for games - synthetic NOT an option. Synthetic competes in municipal/recreational market, NOT professional sports.
