## Company Overview

**KangaTech** is an Australian sports technology company specializing in **isokinetic strength assessment and rehabilitation equipment**. The company's flagship product, **KT360**, is a 360-degree rotational strength testing device that measures maximal push/pull force across multiple directional axes using **7 precision load cells** capable of measuring up to **200 kg of force**.

Unlike traditional wearables (GPS/IMU) or video analysis platforms, KT360 focuses on a narrow but critical niche: **objective strength profiling, bilateral asymmetry detection, and injury rehabilitation tracking** through isokinetic biomechanics assessment.

## Technology & Product Specifications

### KT360 Isokinetic Training Rig - Hardware

**Physical Design**:
- **360° Rotational Platform**: Player stands on circular platform, pushes/pulls against resistance arms in any direction (0-360 degrees)
- **7 Load Cells**: Precision force sensors positioned around platform perimeter measure force output from multiple angles simultaneously
- **Force Capacity**: Up to **200 kg (440 lbs)** maximal force measurement per axis
- **Resistance Modes**: Isokinetic (constant speed, variable resistance), isometric (static hold), eccentric (controlled lengthening)
- **Adjustable Positions**: Height-adjustable arms accommodate players 180-220cm tall (basketball-specific range)
- **Safety Features**: Emergency stop, overload protection, controlled deceleration to prevent injury during maximal efforts

**Dimensions & Installation**:
- **Platform Diameter**: ~1.5-2 meters (estimated, sufficient for basketball player stance)
- **Ceiling Height Required**: 2.8-3.5 meters (arm extension overhead)
- **Floor Space**: 4m × 4m minimum (platform + safety zone + operator station)
- **Power Requirements**: Standard commercial electrical (220-240V)
- **Installation**: Professional setup required (calibration, structural mounting, safety protocols)

### Technology Capabilities

**1. Maximal Strength Profiling**

**Assessment Protocol**:
- **Test**: Player exerts maximal push/pull force in 8 directional axes (0°, 45°, 90°, 135°, 180°, 225°, 270°, 315°)
- **Output**: Force-angle curve showing strength across all directions (spider/radar chart visualization)
- **Metrics**: Peak force (kg), average force, rate of force development (N/s), time to peak

**Use Case (Valencia Basket)**:
- **Pre-Season Baseline**: Test all players (guards, forwards, centers) to establish individual strength profiles
- **Position Comparison**: Compare player strength vs. positional norms (centers higher push force, guards faster rotational power)
- **Longitudinal Tracking**: Retest every 6-8 weeks to measure strength gains from training programs

**2. Bilateral Asymmetry Detection**

**Assessment Protocol**:
- **Test**: Single-leg stance tests (left leg only, right leg only) measuring push/pull force imbalances
- **Output**: Left-right asymmetry percentage (e.g., "Right leg 15% stronger than left")
- **Metrics**: Bilateral force ratio, asymmetry index, compensatory patterns

**Use Case (Valencia Basket - Injury Risk)**:
- **ACL Injury Risk**: >10% quadriceps strength asymmetry = 2.7x ACL injury risk (research-backed threshold)
- **Return-to-Play Criteria**: Post-ACL surgery, player must achieve <10% asymmetry before cleared for full contact
- **Preventive Screening**: Identify high-risk players (>10% asymmetry) → prescribe corrective strength training

**3. Joint-Specific Force Production**

**Assessment Types**:
- **Shoulder Push/Pull**: Horizontal push (chest strength), horizontal pull (upper back strength), overhead push (shoulder strength)
- **Hip Rotation**: Internal/external rotation force (hip mobility + strength, important for defensive slides)
- **Knee Extension/Flexion**: Quadriceps push, hamstring pull (lower limb strength balance)
- **Core Rotation**: Rotational force production (jumping, landing, cutting movements)

**Use Case (Valencia Basket - Strength Prescriptions)**:
- **Example**: Player shows 20% deficit in right hip external rotation vs. left
- **Prescription**: S&C coach assigns targeted hip strengthening exercises (band work, single-leg squats, rotational plyometrics)
- **Retest**: 6 weeks later, asymmetry reduced to 8% (corrective program successful)

**4. Isokinetic Resistance Training**

**Training Modes**:
- **Constant Speed Resistance**: Player pushes against resistance that maintains constant velocity (e.g., 60°/sec rotation)
- **Variable Load**: Resistance automatically adjusts to player's force output (harder they push, more resistance applied)
- **Eccentric Overload**: Controlled lengthening contractions (higher force capacity than concentric, builds strength)

**Use Case (Valencia Basket - High-Performance Training)**:
- **Off-Season Strength Building**: Players perform isokinetic rotational push/pull sets on KT360 (3 sets × 8 reps × 8 directions)
- **Advantage**: Maximal force production throughout full range of motion (vs. free weights where force varies by joint angle)
- **Benefit**: Build rotational power for boxing out, post defense, rebounding battles

**5. Injury Rehabilitation Tracking**

**Rehab Protocol**:
- **Baseline**: Test injured limb strength immediately post-injury (e.g., ACL surgery)
- **Progressive Retesting**: Test every 2-3 weeks during rehab (track strength recovery curve)
- **Return-to-Play Threshold**: Achieve 90%+ strength vs. uninjured limb + <10% bilateral asymmetry
- **Clearance**: Medical staff uses KT360 data to objectively clear player for full training/games

**Use Case (Valencia Basket - ACL Rehabilitation Example)**:
- **Week 0**: Post-surgery, right quad 40% strength vs. left (60% deficit)
- **Week 8**: Right quad 65% strength (35% deficit, progressive improvement)
- **Week 16**: Right quad 85% strength (15% deficit, nearing clearance)
- **Week 20**: Right quad 92% strength (8% deficit, cleared for full contact)

## Competitive Positioning

### KT360 vs. Alternative Strength Assessment Tools

**1. KT360 vs. Force Plates** (e.g., VALD ForceDecks, Hawkin Dynamics)

**Force Plates**:
- **Function**: Dual force plates measure jump height, asymmetry, rate of force development
- **Cost**: €15-25K (cheaper than KT360)
- **Test Type**: Vertical jump only (limited directional assessment)
- **Adoption**: Estimated 4-6 Euroleague teams have force plates (more common than KT360)

**KT360 Advantages**:
- **360° Assessment**: Measures push/pull strength across all directions (vs. force plates = vertical jump only)
- **Rotational Power**: Basketball requires lateral, horizontal, rotational strength (not just vertical jumping)
- **Higher Force Capacity**: 200 kg force measurement (vs. force plates ~100-150 kg typical jump force)

**Force Plate Advantages**:
- **Lower Cost**: €15-25K vs. €40-80K KT360 (2-3x cheaper)
- **Simpler Workflow**: Jump test = 30 seconds (vs. KT360 full assessment = 10-15 minutes)
- **Wider Adoption**: More Euroleague teams have force plates (established norms, benchmarking easier)

**2. KT360 vs. Biodex Isokinetic Dynamometer** (Medical-Grade Alternative)

**Biodex**:
- **Function**: Clinical isokinetic testing (gold standard in sports medicine, hospitals, rehab centers)
- **Cost**: €80-150K (2-3x more expensive than KT360)
- **Test Type**: Single-joint assessment (knee extension/flexion, shoulder rotation)
- **Adoption**: Medical facilities, high-end sports teams (NBA, European football medical departments)

**KT360 Advantages**:
- **Lower Cost**: €40-80K vs. €80-150K Biodex (2x cheaper)
- **Multi-Directional**: 360° rotational testing (vs. Biodex = single-plane movements)
- **Functional Assessment**: Whole-body rotational strength (vs. Biodex = isolated joint testing)

**Biodex Advantages**:
- **Clinical Standard**: Biodex = gold standard for medical rehabilitation (insurance reimbursement, clinical validation)
- **Precision**: Higher measurement accuracy (clinical-grade sensors)
- **Research Database**: Decades of published research, normative databases (KT360 = emerging technology, limited norms)

**3. KT360 vs. Wearables** (e.g., Catapult, WIMU PRO, STATSports)

**Wearables**:
- **Function**: Daily load monitoring (distance, acceleration, jumps, sprint count)
- **Cost**: €30-200K/year (depending on vendor, team size)
- **Data Type**: External load (volume, intensity, workload ratios)
- **Adoption**: Estimated 30-50% of Euroleague teams use wearables

**KT360 Unique Value**:
- **Internal Capacity**: Measures **actual strength** (not external workload) → wearables cannot detect strength deficits or asymmetries
- **Complementary Use**: KT360 + wearables = comprehensive monitoring (wearables track daily load, KT360 assesses strength capacity every 6-8 weeks)
- **Injury Mechanism Insight**: Bilateral asymmetry (KT360) predicts injury risk → wearables detect high load, but cannot identify WHY player is at risk (strength deficit)

**Market Positioning**: KT360 is **NOT** a wearables competitor - it's a **periodic strength assessment tool** (used 6-10 times/season) vs. wearables (used every training session/game). Teams with budgets can adopt BOTH (Valencia may have skipped wearables to afford KT360, or plan to add wearables later).
