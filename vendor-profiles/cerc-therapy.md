## Technology Differentiation

### IPRS vs. Traditional Gym Equipment

**IPRS Advantages**:
- **Functional movements**: Cable-based allows sport-specific patterns (vs. fixed machines)
- **Rehab-performance continuum**: One system for both (vs. separate rehab area + gym)
- **Versatility**: Multiple exercises on one platform (space-efficient)

**IPRS Disadvantages**:
- **Cost**: Specialized system (vs. basic gym equipment)
- **Learning curve**: Staff training required (vs. familiar gym equipment)

### IPRS vs. Manual Therapy (Physiotherapy Tables, Hands-On)

**IPRS**:
- Active rehabilitation (patient performs exercises)
- Measurable loading (resistance quantified)
- Independence (patient can self-train with supervision)

**Manual Therapy**:
- Passive treatment (therapist manipulates patient)
- Complementary to IPRS (use both)
