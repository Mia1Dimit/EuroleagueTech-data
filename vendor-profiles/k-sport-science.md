## Product/Service Portfolio

### 1. Whole-Body Cryotherapy (WBC)

**Cryotherapy Chamber**:
- **Electric or nitrogen-based** chambers (body exposed to -110°C to -140°C for 2-4 minutes)
- **Applications**: Post-game recovery, inflammation reduction, muscle soreness reduction
- **Team use**: Players rotate through chamber post-training/games

**Benefits Claimed**:
- Reduced muscle soreness (DOMS - delayed onset muscle soreness)
- Faster recovery between games
- Inflammation reduction
- Mental alertness/mood enhancement

### 2. Localized Cryotherapy

**Cryo Pen/Wand**:
- Targeted cold therapy for specific body parts (knee, ankle, shoulder, back)
- Uses liquid nitrogen or compressed air
- More intense cold than ice packs (faster treatment)

**Applications**:
- Acute injuries (sprains, contusions)
- Chronic pain points (tendinitis, muscle tightness)
- Pre-treatment for deep tissue massage

### 3. Service & Support

**Installation**:
- Equipment setup at team facility (training center or arena)
- Electrical/nitrogen supply configuration
- Safety training for staff

**Maintenance**:
- Regular servicing of chambers (nitrogen systems, electrical components)
- Safety inspections
- Nitrogen supply coordination (if applicable)

**Training**:
- Medical staff training on cryotherapy protocols
- Safety procedures (contraindications, emergency procedures)
- Optimal usage guidelines (timing, frequency, duration)

## Technology Differentiation

### Whole-Body Cryotherapy vs. Ice Baths

**WBC Advantages**:
- **Extreme cold**: -110°C to -140°C (vs. ice bath ~10-15°C)
- **Time-efficient**: 2-4 minutes (vs. 10-15 minutes in ice bath)
- **Dry cold**: No wet clothing/shivering (vs. submerging in water)
- **Psychological**: Novel modality (players may prefer over ice baths)

**WBC Disadvantages**:
- **Cost**: €40,000-80,000 for chamber + installation (ice bath = €500-2,000)
- **Space**: Requires dedicated room (vs. portable ice bath)
- **Evidence**: Research mixed on superiority over ice baths (some studies show equivalent benefits)
- **Safety**: Requires medical screening (contraindications for heart conditions)

### Localized Cryo vs. Ice Packs

**Localized Cryo Advantages**:
- **Colder temperatures**: More intense than ice packs (faster effect)
- **Targeted**: Specific body part treatment
- **Quick**: 2-5 minutes (vs. 15-20 minutes ice pack)

**Localized Cryo Disadvantages**:
- **Equipment cost**: Device required (vs. free ice)
- **Training needed**: Staff must be trained on use
