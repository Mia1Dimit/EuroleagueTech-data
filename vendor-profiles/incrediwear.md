## Company Overview

**Incrediwear** is a **USA-based recovery apparel company** specializing in **compression garments and sleeves** embedded with **semiconductor elements** claimed to increase blood flow and accelerate recovery. Founded in 2009, Incrediwear markets its products as **"active recovery"** wear—compression garments worn during training, competition, or rest to enhance circulation without the need for electricity or batteries.

**Eywa** appears to be a **brand evolution or partnership** extending Incrediwear's technology into broader wellness/recovery markets.

## Technology: Semiconductor Elements

### Mechanism Claimed

**Embedded Semiconductors** (Carbonized Bamboo Charcoal):
- Fabric infused with **carbon-based semiconductor materials**
- **Claims**: Materials interact with body heat → emit far-infrared energy → vasodilation → increased blood flow
- **No batteries/electricity required**: Passive technology (activated by body heat alone)

**Physiological Effects Claimed**:
- **Increased blood flow**: Enhanced circulation to tissues
- **Reduced inflammation**: Better circulation removes inflammatory mediators
- **Pain relief**: Improved tissue oxygenation, reduced swelling
- **Faster recovery**: Enhanced nutrient delivery, waste removal

### Evidence

**Scientific Basis**:
- **Far-infrared (FIR) textiles**: Some research on FIR-emitting fabrics showing circulation benefits
- **Incrediwear-specific research**: Limited peer-reviewed studies; primarily company-sponsored research
- **User testimonials**: Many athlete endorsements (anecdotal, not controlled trials)

**Skepticism**:
- **Mechanism unclear**: How carbonized bamboo generates FIR in sufficient quantities (physics debated)
- **vs. Compression alone**: Difficult to isolate semiconductor effect from compression effect
- **Placebo possible**: User belief in technology may drive perceived benefits

## Technology Differentiation

### Incrediwear vs. Traditional Compression (CEP, 2XU, Skins)

**Incrediwear**:
- **"Active recovery"**: Semiconductor elements (claimed circulation boost beyond compression)
- **All-day wear**: Marketed for 24/7 use (not just during exercise)
- **Premium pricing**: $40-80/sleeve (higher than basic compression)

**Traditional Compression** (CEP, 2XU, Skins):
- **Compression only**: Graduated compression (tighter at extremities, looser proximal)
- **Proven mechanism**: Compression aids venous return (well-established)
- **Sports performance**: Worn during exercise for support, recovery

**Incrediwear Claim**: Semiconductor tech adds benefit beyond compression alone

### Incrediwear vs. NormaTec/Hyperice (Compression Boots)

**Incrediwear**:
- **Passive compression**: Sleeves/garments (no motors, batteries)
- **Wearable during activity**: Can play/train while wearing
- **Lower cost**: $40-80/product (one-time purchase)

**NormaTec/Hyperice**:
- **Active compression**: Motorized boots (intermittent pneumatic compression)
- **Stationary recovery**: Sit/lie down during use (30-60 min sessions)
- **Higher cost**: $500-1,000+ for devices

**Different use cases**: Incrediwear for continuous wear, NormaTec for dedicated recovery sessions
