## Company Overview

**DJO** (formerly DJO Global, now part of **Enovis Corporation** since 2022) is a **global leader in orthopedic medical devices** specializing in **rehabilitation, pain management, and recovery technologies**. The company manufactures and distributes products for **injury prevention, post-surgical recovery, and pain management** used in hospitals, clinics, professional sports teams, and home care settings worldwide.

### Corporate Structure

**Enovis Acquisition** (2022):
- Colfax Corporation acquired DJO Global for **$3.15 billion**
- Combined with other medical device assets to form **Enovis Corporation** (NYSE: ENOV)
- DJO operates as a brand/division within Enovis
- Portfolio includes: DJO, Aircast, Chattanooga, Compex, CMF (craniomaxillofacial)

### Market Position

## Product Portfolio

### 1. Cold Therapy & Compression (Flagship Products)

**Game Ready** (Acquired 2017):
- **Cold + Compression therapy system**
- Applications: Post-surgery, acute injuries, recovery
- Technology: Intermittent pneumatic compression + circulating ice water
- Wraps available for: Knee, ankle, shoulder, hip, elbow, back
- Used by professional sports teams worldwide (NFL, NBA, NHL, European soccer)

**Aircast Cryo/Cuff**:
- Simpler cold + compression system (gravity-fed, not motorized)
- More affordable than Game Ready
- Portable, easy to use
- Popular in clinics, smaller teams, home use

### 2. Bracing & Supports

**Knee Braces**:
- **DonJoy Defiance** - Custom ACL braces (post-surgery protection)
- **DonJoy Armor** - Off-the-shelf functional knee braces
- **Reaction Web** - Knee stabilization for mild instabilities
- **Patella straps** - Patellar tendinopathy support

**Ankle Braces**:
- **Aircast A60** - Prophylactic ankle brace (injury prevention)
- **Aircast Stirrup** - Acute ankle sprain treatment
- Lace-up ankle braces for chronic instability

**Other Braces**:
- Back supports (lumbar braces)
- Shoulder braces (post-surgery, instability)
- Wrist/elbow braces (tendinitis, sprains)

### 3. Electrotherapy & Recovery (Compex Brand)

**Compex** (Acquired 2011):
- **Electrical muscle stimulation (EMS)** devices
- Applications: Recovery, warm-up, pain relief, strength training
- Wireless units with electrode pads
- Popular in European sports (cycling, soccer, rugby)

**Chattanooga** (Acquired 1996):
- **Professional electrotherapy** equipment for clinics
- Ultrasound therapy units
- TENS (transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation) for pain
- Laser therapy devices

### 4. Rehabilitation Equipment

**Continuous Passive Motion (CPM)** Machines:
- Post-surgical knee/shoulder rehab
- Maintains joint range of motion after surgery
- Used in hospitals, rehab centers

**Traction Devices**:
- Cervical/lumbar traction for spine decompression
- Pain management for herniated discs, nerve compression

### 5. Surgical Implants (CMF Division)

**Craniomaxillofacial** (CMF):
- Plates, screws for facial/skull surgery
- Not relevant to basketball (trauma/orthognathic surgery focus)

## Technology Differentiation

### Game Ready vs. Traditional Ice Baths

**Game Ready Advantages**:
- **Targeted therapy**: Specific joint (knee, ankle, shoulder) vs. full-body ice bath
- **Compression + cold**: Dual modality (ice bath is cold only)
- **Consistent temperature**: Circulating ice water maintains cold (ice bath warms up)
- **Convenience**: Player sits/lies comfortably (vs. submerging in cold water)

**Game Ready Disadvantages**:
- **Cost**: $3,000-5,000/unit (ice bath = $500-2,000)
- **Not full-body**: Ice bath treats entire lower body, Game Ready treats one joint at a time

### Game Ready vs. Normatec (Hyperice Compression)

**Game Ready**:
- Cold + compression (dual modality)
- Medical-grade (FDA cleared as Class II medical device)
- Post-surgery, acute injury focus

**Normatec**:
- Compression only (no cold)
- Wellness/fitness focus (not medical device)
- Legs, arms, hips (full-limb compression)

**Overlap**: Both used for recovery, but different mechanisms (cold vs. compression alone)

### DJO Braces vs. Competitors (McDavid, Zamst, Bauerfeind)

**DJO Advantages**:
- **Custom bracing**: DonJoy Defiance (custom-molded ACL braces)
- **Medical pedigree**: Used in orthopedic clinics, hospitals (credibility)
- **Research-backed**: Clinical studies on DonJoy braces

**DJO Disadvantages**:
- **Cost**: Premium pricing (vs. McDavid off-the-shelf)
- **Lead time**: Custom braces require fitting, manufacturing (vs. immediate off-the-shelf)

**Compex EMS Units**:

  - Market access (credibility in European basketball)
- **Estimated value exchange**: $25-50k/year (equipment + consumables)

**Intangible Benefits**:
